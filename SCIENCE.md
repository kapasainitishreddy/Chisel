# Chisel — SCIENCE.md

**No method without a receipt.** Every claim shown in the app carries an evidence
strength — **Strong / Moderate / Limited / None (myth)** — and maps to a citation
below. Where the evidence is weak or absent, the app says so on the card itself.
Chisel is cosmetic & educational guidance, not medical advice.

## Evidence tiers

| Tier | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Strong** | RCT(s), systematic review, or unambiguous clinical guidance |
| **Moderate** | Controlled studies or consistent clinical literature, smaller/emerging |
| **Limited** | Plausible mechanism, weak or indirect evidence — framed as optional |
| **None (myth)** | No controlled evidence; labeled as a myth in-app |

## Honesty rules baked into the product

1. Measure only what a phone camera can measure; everything else is a **guided
   self-check**, clearly labeled "no fake AI score."
2. Myths are shown *with* their myth label, not hidden — users learn what
   doesn't work (mewing, lip exercises, nose exercises, beard rollers).
3. Referrals where an app must stop: dermatologist (skin/hair), dentist
   (alignment), facial plastic surgeon (structure).
4. Progress framing ("skin evenness ▲ +6") over ranking; no comparison to
   other people, ever.
